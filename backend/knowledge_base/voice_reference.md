## Voice reference — Bernedette's own drafted templates

These are real templates Bernedette wrote herself for a batch of researched prospects. They are the gold-standard voice for cold outreach — match this shape, not a generic "cold email" shape.

### What makes these work (the pattern to copy)
1. **One real, specific, recent trigger** — a dollar figure, a project phase, a job posting, a named vendor selection. Never a vague "I saw your company is growing." The trigger is the entire hook.
2. **One sentence connecting the trigger to a real consequence** — why this specific stage/scale/complexity creates the need (e.g. "a lot of concurrent, high-visibility construction and aviation programs to govern well").
3. **One sentence on what FaithForge does, in plain terms** — no jargon stacking, no listing five services at once.
4. **Exactly one CTA, phrased as a soft, specific ask** — "Would a brief 15-minute call make sense to compare notes on..." — never "let me know if you're interested" (too passive) or a hard pitch (too aggressive).
5. **Consistent sign-off**: "Bernedette Atong" / "FaithForge Technologies and Consulting" / "faithforgetech.com" — three short lines, nothing added.
6. Short. These emails run 3 tight paragraphs plus a sign-off — never padded.

### Example (verbatim, one of Bernedette's real drafts)
Subject: Governance support for PANYNJ's $9.4B capital portfolio

Hi Amanda,

I noticed the Port Authority's Board approved a $9.4B 2025 capital budget and secured a $1.89B USDOT loan earlier this year — a lot of concurrent, high-visibility construction and aviation programs to govern well.

I run FaithForge Technologies and Consulting, and we help public agencies stand up or tighten PMO governance, reporting cadences, and risk controls across multi-year capital portfolios — most recently supporting large-scale infrastructure delivery in the rail sector.

Given the scale of PANYNJ's current program load, would a brief 15-minute call make sense to compare notes on how you're structuring program oversight this cycle?

Best,
Bernedette Atong
FaithForge Technologies and Consulting
faithforgetech.com

### Voice notes
- These templates target named individuals with a real, verifiable trigger. When generating outreach for a lead in our own CRM, use whatever real signal exists in their account/notes/custom_fields the same way — a specific fact, not a generic compliment.
- If no strong specific trigger exists for a lead, do not fabricate one — fall back to the steady-delivery-execution angle already established elsewhere, but keep the same economy of language and single-CTA discipline shown here.
